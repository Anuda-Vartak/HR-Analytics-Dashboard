# HR-Analytics-Dashboard

## HR Analytics: Employee Attrition Insights Dashboard
A dynamic, interactive data visualization tool built to explore and analyze employee attrition trends across departments, demographics, salary bands, and job roles.

## Short Description
The HR Analytics Dashboard is a visually engaging Power BI report designed to help HR teams and business leaders monitor workforce health across 731 employees. It focuses on identifying attrition patterns by age, gender, education, salary, and job role — empowering data-driven decisions to improve employee retention.

## Tech Stack
The dashboard was built using the following tools and technologies:<br>
📊 Power BI Desktop – Main data visualization platform.<br>
📂 Power Query – Data transformation and cleaning.<br>
🧠 DAX – Calculated measures and dynamic visuals.<br>
📁 File Format – .pbix for development, .png for previews.<br>

## Features/Highlights
### Problem Statement
HR teams often struggle to identify why and who is leaving the organization. Without clear visual insights, retention strategies remain reactive rather than proactive.

### Goal of the Dashboard
To deliver an interactive tool that uncovers attrition patterns and helps HR teams take targeted action.

### Key Visuals
-KPIs – Total employees (731), Attrition (136), Attrition Rate (18.6%), Avg Age, Salary & Tenure
-Attrition by Gender – Male vs Female breakdown.<br>
-Attrition by Age – Highest attrition in the 26–35 group.<br>
-Attrition by Salary – Majority leaving earn under 5K.<br>
-Attrition by Job Role – Lab Technicians and Sales Executives most affected.<br>
-Attrition by Education – Life Sciences leads at 20%.<br>
-Attrition by Years – Tenure-based trend analysis.<br>

### Business Impact
-Helps HR identify high-risk employee segments.<br>
-Supports targeted retention strategies by role and salary band.<br>
-Enables department-level filtering across HR, R&D, and Sales.<br>

## 🔍 Walkthrough of Key Visuals

- **Key KPIs (Top Section)**
  Total Employees: 731 | Attrition: 136 | Attrition Rate: 18.6% | Avg Age: 35.85 | Avg Salary: 5.6K | Avg Tenure: 5 Years — giving a quick snapshot of overall workforce health at a glance.

- **Department Filter Panel**
  An interactive slicer lets users filter all visuals by selected departments — Human Resources, Research & Development, and Sales.

- **Attrition by Gender (Top Right)**
  A visual comparison showing male (136) vs female (76) attrition, highlighting gender-based workforce trends.

- **Attrition by Education (Donut Chart)**
  Breaks down attrition by educational background — Life Sciences leads at 20%, followed by Medical (16%), Marketing (8%), and Technical Degree (10%).

- **Attrition by Age (Bar Chart)**
  Bar chart ranking attrition across age groups. The 26–35 group has the highest attrition (67), followed by 18–25 (25), helping identify the most at-risk age segment.

- **Attrition by Salary (Horizontal Bar Chart)**
  Displays attrition across salary bands. Employees earning up to 5K account for the majority (99), indicating salary as a key attrition driver.

- **Attrition by Job Role (Horizontal Bar Chart)**
  Ranks job roles by attrition count — Laboratory Technicians (45), Sales Executives (30), and Research Scientists (28) are the most affected roles.

- **Attrition by Job Role & Satisfaction (Matrix Table)**
  A detailed table breaking down attrition by job role and satisfaction level (1–4), helping identify dissatisfaction patterns within specific roles.

- **Attrition by Years (Area/Line Chart)**
  Trend line showing attrition across employee tenure. Peaks are visible in early years, suggesting onboarding and early engagement are critical retention points.

  ## 💡 Business Impact & Insights

- **Retention Strategy:** HR teams can identify high-risk employee segments by age, salary, and job role — enabling targeted retention initiatives before attrition escalates.

- **Compensation Planning:** The salary-based attrition breakdown reveals that employees earning under 5K are most likely to leave, helping leadership prioritize compensation reviews.

- **Role-Specific Interventions:** Insights into job roles like Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives allow managers to design role-specific engagement and career growth programs.

- **Early Tenure Focus:** Attrition peaks in early years highlight the need for stronger onboarding, mentorship, and early engagement programs to retain new hires.

- **Diversity & Inclusion:** Gender-based attrition data helps HR monitor and improve equity in retention across male and female employees.

- **Department-Level Decision Making:** Department filters allow HR business partners to drill into specific teams — HR, R&D, or Sales — and take focused action rather than applying one-size-fits-all policies.
